# The Collateral

**In a closing, collateral is what gets seized when the deal goes bad.**

---

## You.

---

Not the abstraction. Not "the American people" — the phrase that politicians use when they mean no one in particular. You. The person reading this.

The gas you bought this morning is priced by the [Strait of Hormuz closure](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/3/3/shutdown-of-hormuz-strait-raises-fears-of-soaring-oil-prices) described in Part I. The grocery bill you'll pay this week moves with the oil price that moved with the war that moved with the deal. Your 401(k) — if you have one — is a number on a screen that [Goldman Sachs just told you has a 25% chance of entering a recession](https://www.axios.com/2026/03/12/oil-prices-iran-strait-of-hormuz), because a non-congressionally-authorized war closed a strait that moves 21% of the world's oil, to pay a debt you were never told about, on terms you were never consulted on.

If you have a child in the military, they are deployed in a theater where a country that is [providing targeting intelligence to the enemy](https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/2026/03/06/russia-iran-intelligence-us-targets/) just [received sanctions relief](https://thehill.com/policy/energy-environment/5782247-trump-lifts-russian-oil-sanctions/) from their commander-in-chief. [Six service members are dead](https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/06/politics/russia-aiding-iran-targeting) in Kuwait. The administration called the intelligence-sharing ["not really a factor."](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/7/us-downplays-reports-russia-gave-iran-intel-to-help-tehran-strike-us-assets) If your child is next, that is the official position.

If you voted, your vote is the subject of a [draft executive order](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/trump-says-hes-not-mulling-a-draft-executive-order-to-seize-control-over-elections-heres-what-we-know) that would give the president emergency power over the election machinery. If you plan to vote in November, the mechanisms that would count that vote are being degraded by the same people whose positions depend on that vote not producing oversight. Your participation in democracy is not a right being protected. It is a variable being managed.

If you pay taxes, the IRS enforcement capacity that ensures the wealthy pay theirs has been cut to a level projected to [lose $2.4 trillion over ten years](https://democrats-budget.house.gov/resources/report/doges-mass-firings-result-gutted-services-and-higher-costs). The efficiency operation costs more than it saves. The difference comes from your share of the public account — or from services you depend on that will no longer be funded.

You are the collateral. You have been the collateral for the entire series. Every settlement described in Parts I through V was paid from your account — in security, in economic stability, in democratic function, in the lives of people who serve in your name. The parties at the table did not ask your permission. They did not disclose the terms. You were not at the table. You were on it.

This piece documents the cost. Not in the aggregate. In the specific.

## I. The blood

On March 6, 2026, an [Iranian drone killed six American service members](https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/06/politics/russia-aiding-iran-targeting) in Kuwait. Their names have been released to their families. Their families have been told they died serving their country.

They did. But the country they were serving had, six days later, [lifted sanctions](https://thehill.com/policy/energy-environment/5782247-trump-lifts-russian-oil-sanctions/) on the government that provided the targeting intelligence used to find them. The sequence documented in Part I is not abstract for these families. It is the specific mechanism by which their children were located and killed — and the specific decision by their government to reward the country that made it possible.

Russia [provided Iran with satellite imagery](https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/2026/03/06/russia-iran-intelligence-us-targets/) identifying the locations of American warships and aircraft. [CNN confirmed](https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/06/politics/russia-aiding-iran-targeting) the intelligence included targeting data. The service members in Kuwait were within the targeting envelope that Russian intelligence helped define. Six of them are dead.

The administration's position is that the intelligence-sharing is ["not really a factor"](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/7/us-downplays-reports-russia-gave-iran-intel-to-help-tehran-strike-us-assets) in the bilateral relationship with Russia. The sanctions relief proceeded. The Kremlin's investment envoy [celebrated publicly](https://www.irishtimes.com/world/us/2026/03/13/us-eases-russia-oil-sanctions-as-iran-war-pushes-up-energy-prices/). The families received flags.

This is what collateral looks like when it has a pulse. The troops are not a line item. They are people who were sent into a theater of war that was created to serve the terms of a deal they knew nothing about — and who died because a country that is ostensibly not the enemy provided the information that killed them, and was rewarded for it.

The number directly tied to Russian intelligence-sharing is six. The total American casualties from the Iran theater are higher and climbing. The war is ongoing. The Strait of Hormuz is still closed. The theater is still active. The intelligence-sharing has not stopped. Both numbers will change.

## II. The economy

The reader does not need a finance degree to understand what is happening to their cost of living. They need a gas receipt.

Oil was approximately $60 per barrel before Operation Epic Fury. It is approximately $100 now. [Oxford Economics has identified $140](https://www.axios.com/2026/03/12/oil-prices-iran-strait-of-hormuz) as the threshold at which a global recession becomes likely. [Goldman Sachs has raised the probability of a U.S. recession to 25%](https://www.axios.com/2026/03/12/oil-prices-iran-strait-of-hormuz). The [IEA has authorized the release of 400 million barrels](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/11/irans-irgc-says-not-one-litre-of-oil-will-get-through-strait-of-hormuz) from strategic reserves — but authorization is not delivery. Strategic reserves cannot be emptied on command. They are constrained by physical infrastructure: pipeline capacity, pumping rates, the fluid dynamics of salt cavern storage. The U.S. Strategic Petroleum Reserve can deliver approximately 4.4 million barrels per day at maximum drawdown. The global shortfall from the Hormuz closure is approximately 17 million barrels per day. The reserve is a buffer, not a replacement — and once depleted, it takes years to refill at current production rates. The math does not close.

These are not numbers that happen to you gradually. They happen at the pump, at the grocery store, in your heating bill, in the price of everything that moves by truck — which is everything. The mechanism is direct: a war closed a strait, the strait closure raised oil prices, the oil price increase is transmitted through the entire supply chain to the consumer. You. Every dollar of that increase is a tax you did not vote for, levied to fund a war you were not consulted on, to serve creditors you were not told about.

The tariff regime compounds the damage. The same administration that claims market stabilization as the rationale for the sanctions relief is simultaneously imposing tariffs that economists identify as inflationary. The consumer absorbs both — the energy shock from the war and the price increases from the trade policy. The cumulative effect is a cost-of-living increase that functions as a regressive tax: it hits hardest at the bottom, where energy and food consume the largest share of income.

The administration's metric for economic health is the stock market. The Dow. The Nasdaq. Numbers that measure the wealth of people who own assets — not the cost borne by people who buy groceries. Attorney General Bondi, when asked about prosecuting Epstein's co-conspirators, talked about the Dow hitting 50,000. The Dow is not your economy. Your economy is the spread between what you earn and what it costs to live. That spread is shrinking, and the causes are documented in every preceding installment of this series.

## III. The dead we cannot name

Part I documented a timeline. Classified documents identifying [human intelligence sources](https://www.npr.org/2022/08/29/1119889338/after-mar-a-lago-officials-are-analyzing-the-potential-damage-to-national-securi) — HCS material, the most sensitive category in the intelligence community — were stored unsecured at a resort club. The compromise pattern may have begun during the first term, when the president had lawful access to the same material. Nine months after the documents arrived at Mar-a-Lago, the CIA [sent an unprecedented cable](https://theweek.com/foreign-affairs/1005694/unusual-cable-specifies-that-dozens-of-cia-informants-have-been-arrested) to every station on earth warning that a troubling number of informants were being [captured, killed, or compromised](https://www.realcleardefense.com/2021/10/06/captured_killed_or_compromised_cia_losing_dozens_of_informants_797676.html).

Those informants were people. They had families. They took risks — often at the request of American intelligence officers who promised them that the system would protect their identities. The system stored their identities in a basement, a closet, and a former dressing room at a resort where a [Russian-speaking woman with a fake identity](https://www.cornellpolicyreview.com/the-executive-records-recovered-from-mar-a-lago-and-the-c-i-a-s-missing-informants/?pdf=6365) was photographed with the president.

The [damage assessment](https://www.justsecurity.org/86887/national-security-implications-of-trumps-indictment-a-damage-assessment/) has never been made public. The charges were dropped. The classification authority that keeps the answer sealed belongs to the man whose residence was searched. These are people who cannot be named — even now, even posthumously — because naming them would compromise the methods used to recruit them and endanger others still in the field.

This is the collateral that weighs the most and makes the least sound.

## IV. The ballot

Your vote is the one instrument in this system that was designed to be yours. Every other mechanism described in this series — the financial flows, the intelligence equities, the donor pipelines, the leverage apparatus — operates above, below, or around you. The vote is the single point at which you are supposed to have a say.

Parts III and V documented what is being done to that instrument. The president has [floated canceling the midterm elections](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/17/politics/midterm-elections-trump-2026-analysis) — twice. A [17-page draft executive order](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/trump-says-hes-not-mulling-a-draft-executive-order-to-seize-control-over-elections-heres-what-we-know) proposing emergency powers over election machinery has been confirmed by PBS. The [Civil Rights Division has been redirected](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/17/politics/midterm-elections-trump-2026-analysis) from protecting voting rights to purging voter rolls. Federal courts have [blocked the most aggressive provisions](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/judge-rules-trump-cant-require-u-s-citizenship-proof-on-federal-voting-form) and will likely continue to do so. [Election experts are unanimous](https://www.votebeat.org/2026/01/26/why-trump-cant-cancel-2026-midterm-elections/) that the president cannot legally cancel elections.

But the hedge does not require canceling the election. It requires degrading the conditions under which the election produces accountability. If enough procedural chaos is introduced — re-registration requirements, machine changes, legal challenges, ICE presence at polling places — the election can occur and still fail to deliver the oversight that would unwind the settlements.

Your vote is collateral because the parties to the deal need it to be worth less than its face value. Not void. Not stolen. Just degraded enough that the result doesn't produce a majority with subpoena power, investigative authority, and the will to use both. The 37 missing pages stay missing. The damage assessment stays classified. The financial arrangements stay opaque. And the deal continues.

The midterms are in November. Between now and then, every element of the hedge documented in Part V will be tested against the election machinery that 9,000 local jurisdictions and 90,000 polling locations are committed to operating. The question is not whether you can vote. The question is whether your vote, once cast, produces the institutional consequence it was designed to produce.

## V. The allies

The collateral extends beyond American borders — but if you are an American reading this, it comes back to you. The alliances that protect your security, your trade relationships, your position in the world — they are being spent down to fund the same closing.

Ukraine is fighting a war against a country whose isolation the [Alaska summit](https://www.npr.org/2025/08/17/g-s1-83183/putin-trump-ceasefire) ended, whose oil revenue the [sanctions relief](https://thehill.com/policy/energy-environment/5782247-trump-lifts-russian-oil-sanctions/) restored, and whose territorial gains the administration has tacitly accepted. The framework that emerged from Alaska — that a comprehensive settlement must precede any ceasefire — allows Russia to continue fighting indefinitely. Ukraine's position has deteriorated since August. The leverage that sanctions provided has been traded for the energy stabilization that the administration itself made necessary by launching the Iran war. If you are wondering why the United States spent a decade building a coalition to contain Russian expansion and is now unwinding it in months, the preceding five installments of this series are the answer.

European allies are absorbing the economic shock of the Hormuz closure alongside you — but without having been consulted on the operation that caused it. NATO's foundational premise is collective security through consultation. [Operation Epic Fury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis) was launched bilaterally, by the United States and Israel, without NATO consultation, creating consequences that every member state now bears. The energy crisis is not an American problem. It is a global one — created by an American decision, for reasons no allied government was given.

The security architecture that has kept the postwar order stable for eighty years is not being dismantled by an adversary. It is being traded by the country that built it — in exchange for the terms of a closing that serves two foreign creditors and a president who cannot afford to leave office. You may not live in Kyiv or Brussels or Taipei. But the security commitments that were made on your behalf, in your name, with your tax dollars — those are on the table too. And you were not consulted.

The allies are collateral because the promises the United States made to them were made by a different country — one that had not yet traded those commitments for the terms of a deal. The promises are still on paper. The paper is worth what the parties at the table decide it is worth. And neither you nor the allies are at the table.

## VI. The deal ends badly either way

This is the section the reader has been waiting for — and the one that offers no comfort.

The preceding installments presented, implicitly, a binary: the closing succeeds or it fails. If it succeeds, the creditors collect, the exposure is buried, and the arrangement becomes permanent. If it fails, oversight arrives, the files come out, and accountability begins. You have likely been tracking toward one outcome or the other — hoping for the second, fearing the first.

Both outcomes damage you.

**If the closing succeeds:**

The settlements documented in Part I become permanent. Russian sanctions relief becomes the baseline. The energy architecture is restructured around Russian supply. The financial arrangements — the sovereign wealth fund payments, the investment vehicles, the deal flow managed by the Kremlin's envoy — are never investigated. The files stay suppressed. The damage assessment stays classified. The 34 felony convictions are never executed. The co-conspirator's clemency resolves quietly. The midterms either don't function or don't produce oversight. The institutional capacity to investigate has been removed by the hedge documented in Part V.

You live in a country whose foreign policy is permanently shaped by the leverage documented in this series. The creditors do not stop collecting because the closing is complete. The leverage is perpetual. Kompromat does not expire. Every concession produces the expectation of another. The deal does not end. It becomes the operating system. And every future policy decision — every alliance, every trade agreement, every military deployment — is made by a government whose decision-makers are compromised in ways you cannot see because the mechanisms of disclosure have been dismantled.

The cost to you is invisible and permanent. You do not notice the policy that was not made, the investigation that was not opened, the alliance that was not honored, the intelligence that was not shared. You notice only the cumulative erosion — the sense that the government is not working for you, without being able to identify the specific mechanism by which it was redirected. The most effective form of control is not the boot on the face. It is the slow adjustment of what is considered normal — the incremental acceptance that things have always been this way, that the institutions were never going to protect you, that the erosion is just how the world works.

*"The ideal subject of totalitarian rule is not the convinced Nazi or the convinced Communist, but people for whom the distinction between fact and fiction and the distinction between true and false no longer exists."*

Arendt wrote that about the mid-twentieth century. It describes the twenty-first with greater precision than she could have intended — not a population that has been forbidden from knowing the truth, but one that has been buried in so much noise, so many competing narratives, so many daily outrages each displacing the last, that the truth is not hidden. It is drowned. That is what a successful closing looks like from the outside. Not a secret kept. A signal lost — in a world where the institutions that were supposed to amplify it no longer function, because the people who needed the signal to stay lost are the same people who dismantled the amplifiers.

**If the closing fails:**

Oversight arrives. The subpoenas issue. The files come out. The financial arrangements are exposed. The damage assessment is declassified. The 37 pages are produced. The scope of what has been done — the intelligence compromise, the foreign leverage, the policy concessions, the suppression — becomes public.

And then what?

The institutional damage documented in Part V has already occurred. The [317,000 federal employees](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-12-10/federal-workforce-s-toll-after-a-year-of-doge-and-trump-317-000) are gone. The inspectors general are gone. The DOJ's [Public Integrity Section is gutted](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/department-justices-broken-accountability-system). The career staff who maintained institutional memory have been replaced by political appointees whose loyalties are to the arrangement, not the institution. The judiciary has been reshaped. The intelligence community's equities in the broader function — whatever they are — have been protected by classification for decades and will not be surrendered easily.

The exposure of the closing does not restore the institutions. It reveals that they were hollowed out — and that the hollowing was the point. You learn the scope of the compromise and simultaneously discover that the machinery that would have addressed it has been dismantled. The revelation and the incapacity arrive together.

You learn that the security commitments your country made to its allies were traded. You learn that intelligence sources your agencies recruited were compromised. You learn that the institutional infrastructure your markets trusted was captured. The credibility damage is not limited to one administration. It extends to the institutions themselves — the ones that were supposed to prevent this and didn't, across multiple administrations, across both parties, for decades.

The cost to you is visible and destabilizing. You learn what was done. You learn that no institution stopped it. You learn that both parties were at the table, constrained by the structures documented in Part IV. And you learn that the mechanisms you were told would protect you — elections, oversight, the courts, the press, the intelligence community — either failed or were captured. The question that follows is not "how do we fix this?" It is "what do we trust?" And the answer, for a period that no one can predict, is: less than you did before you knew.

## The cost is the cost

This is not a series that ends with a call to action, because a call to action implies that the system has a lever the reader can pull. Parts I through V documented the systematic neutralization of every such lever. The vote is the one that remains — and Part V documented what is being done to degrade it.

The collateral is real. The blood is real. The economic cost is real. The democratic degradation is real. The intelligence compromise is real. None of it is recoverable in full. Some of it is not recoverable at all. The six service members are not coming back. The informants who were compromised are not coming back. The institutional memory carried out in bankers' boxes by 317,000 federal employees is not coming back. The cost has been paid. It was paid from your account. And no outcome described in this piece makes you whole.

---

But.

A closing is not complete until every party has delivered. The settlements described in this series are being executed, but they are not finished. The midterms have not been canceled. The courts have not been fully captured. The files are still coming out. The 37 pages have not been produced, but they have not been destroyed — as far as anyone knows. The damage assessment exists, even if it is classified.

The election machinery is still operated by [9,000 local jurisdictions](https://www.votebeat.org/2026/01/26/why-trump-cant-cancel-2026-midterm-elections/) committed to running it. Courts have [blocked the most aggressive provisions](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/judge-rules-trump-cant-require-u-s-citizenship-proof-on-federal-voting-form). Career officials — the ones who remain — are still processing the files, still managing the systems, still doing the work that the political layer above them is trying to redirect. The press broke the story of the missing pages. [NPR forced the release](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/05/nx-s1-5737562/justice-department-missing-epstein-files-trump) of 16 of them. The [bipartisan investigation](https://www.npr.org/2026/02/25/nx-s1-5726780/democrats-doj-epstein-files-trump-missing-pages) is underway. Thomas Massie is still on the House floor, naming names, demanding answers, refusing to stop.

The collateral has been seized. The question is whether anything can be recovered — and whether you will act on the knowledge of what was done to you, while the one instrument you still hold has not yet been neutralized.

You were never at the table. You have always been on it.

What you do with that knowledge is the only variable in this closing that the parties at the table do not control.
